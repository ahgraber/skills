# Attributions and Citations

Adapted from Geoffrey Litt's [`explain-diff` prompts](https://gist.github.com/geoffreylitt/a29df1b5f9865506e8952488eac3d524), which define the Background / Intuition / Code / Quiz structure, the self-contained dated HTML output, and the Martin-Kleppmann-clarity style directive.
The gist's comment thread (Butanium) flagged that correct quiz answers were guessable for being the longest option — generalized here into the item-writing rules in `references/quiz-design.md`.

## Research informing the design

Grounds the design in learning science; each entry maps to a decision in the skill's Section Guide.

### Interactivity and active learning

- Chi & Wylie (2014), ICAP framework — Interactive > Constructive > Active > Passive. <https://doi.org/10.1080/00461520.2014.965823>
- Freeman et al. (2014), active learning meta-analysis (225 studies). <https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1319030111>
- Chi et al. (1994), self-explanation beats re-reading. <https://doi.org/10.1016/0364-0213(94)90016-7>
- Dunlosky et al. (2013), effective learning techniques (testing, spacing, elaboration). <https://doi.org/10.1177/1529100612453266>

### Prior knowledge, scaffolding, expertise gating

- Ausubel (1968) / Bryce & Blown (2024), advance organizers, activate prior knowledge. <https://doi.org/10.1007/s12144-023-04440-4>
- Wood, Bruner & Ross (1976), scaffolding; Vygotsky (1978), zone of proximal development. <https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1469-7610.1976.tb00381.x>
- Kalyuga et al. (2003), Kalyuga (2007), expertise-reversal effect — justifies the skippable-for-experts Background split. <https://doi.org/10.1207/S15326985EP3801_4>

### Cognitive load, worked examples, concreteness

- Sweller (1988), Sweller/van Merriënboer/Paas (1998), cognitive load — chunk the walkthrough. <https://doi.org/10.1023/A:1022193728205>
- Sweller & Cooper (1985), worked-example effect. <https://doi.org/10.1207/s1532690xci0201_3>
- Goldstone & Son (2005), Fyfe et al. (2014), concreteness fading — toy example first, then abstract. <https://doi.org/10.1007/s10648-014-9249-3>

### Multimedia and dual coding (diagrams)

- Paivio (1986), dual coding; Mayer (2009), words + pictures beat words alone. <https://doi.org/10.1017/CBO9780511811678>
- Fiorella & Mayer (2014), coherence/signaling/contiguity — cut decorative visuals, keep labels adjacent. <https://doi.org/10.1017/CBO9781139547369.015>

### Quiz: retrieval practice, feedback, item writing

- Roediger & Karpicke (2006), Roediger & Butler (2011), testing effect. <https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1467-9280.2006.01693.x>
- Hattie & Timperley (2007), power of feedback — explain every option. <https://doi.org/10.3102/003465430298487>
- Bjork & Bjork (2011), desirable difficulties — effortful but answerable. <https://bjorklab.psych.ucla.edu/wp-content/uploads/sites/13/2016/04/EBjork_RBjork_2011.pdf>
- Haladyna, Downing & Rodriguez (2002), multiple-choice item-writing guidelines. <https://doi.org/10.1207/S15324818AME1503_5>
- Krathwohl (2002), revised Bloom's taxonomy — target understand/apply. <https://doi.org/10.1207/s15430421tip4104_2>

### Spacing and developer-education context

- Cepeda et al. (2006), distributed practice — supports dated, revisitable artifacts. <https://doi.org/10.1037/0033-2909.132.3.354>
- Brooks (1983), Storey (2005), program comprehension is top-down then bottom-up. <https://doi.org/10.1109/WPC.2005.38>
- Bacchelli & Bird (2013), code review's main value is knowledge transfer. <https://doi.org/10.1109/ICSE.2013.6606617>
- Steinmacher et al. (2015), newcomer barriers are missing context. <https://doi.org/10.1016/j.infsof.2014.11.001>
- Morrison, Margulieux & Guzdial (2015), subgoal-labeled worked examples aid transfer. <https://doi.org/10.1145/2787622.2787733>
- Nam et al. (2024), LLM explanations aid code understanding (emerging, with caveats). <https://doi.org/10.1145/3597503.3639187>
