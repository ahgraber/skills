# Correspondence register

Apply alongside the core directives for email and letters written to a person.
The core still governs — lead with the point, cut filler, be specific.
What changes is that a message has a sender, a recipient, and a relationship, so some things the baseline strips are expected courtesy here.

## The target

A message a capable colleague would send: clear, warm enough for the relationship, and quick to the ask.
It respects the reader's time.
It is not a form letter, and it is not an essay wearing an email's clothes.

## Deltas from the core

The ask leads.
Put the request, decision, or purpose in the first line or two, and in the subject line when there is one — the reader should know what you want before they scroll.
Close the same way, on the next step and who owns it ("I'll send the draft Friday; flag anything before then"), not a generic "looking forward to it."

Greetings, sign-offs, and warmth are the form of the medium, not slop.
"Hi Priya," and "Thanks, Sam" are expected; keep them, and match their warmth to the relationship.
Correspondence sits lower on the formality scale than a report, so contractions and a natural spoken voice are correct here.
Match the register to the reader as well: a note to your manager, a client, and a friend differ in formality, warmth, and length.

Some "chatbot phrases" are ordinary courtesy — judge by sincerity.
"Let me know if that works" or a brief "thanks for the quick turnaround" is normal email, not assistant leakage.
The tell is the empty reflexive version: "Great question!", "I hope this email finds you well" to a close colleague, praise that means nothing.
Keep the courtesy a person would actually write; cut the boilerplate.

## What still fails here

Padding an opener to someone you know still fails ("I hope this finds you well," "I wanted to reach out to you regarding").
Significance inflation and promotional tone still fail — a routine update need not be momentous.
Burying the ask under context still fails; the background goes below the request.
And over-hedging a simple request into three qualifiers still fails.
